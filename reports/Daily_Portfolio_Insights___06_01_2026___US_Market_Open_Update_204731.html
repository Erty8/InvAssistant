Macro & Sector Risk Assessment

The prevailing theme across our portfolio remains the robust expansion of the Artificial Intelligence and Semiconductor sectors. Today's performance underscores the market's continued appetite for companies at the forefront of this technological revolution. However, the significant number of tickers (7 out of 11) now registering in overbought RSI territory is a critical signal. While strong momentum can persist, these levels often precede periods of consolidation or modest pullbacks as investors digest gains and re-evaluate valuations.

We observe a concentration of bullish sentiment and technical strength within AI-related names, from infrastructure providers like CRWV and ORCL to chipmakers like MRVL and MU, and even AI-leveraged platforms like NBIS and ZETA. This concentration, while rewarding in a bull market, inherently carries sector-specific risk. Any broader market shift in sentiment towards AI, or specific regulatory/competitive pressures, could disproportionately impact these holdings. The lack of high-volume spikes suggests the rally is more of a steady accumulation rather than a climactic buying event, which could imply more sustainable, albeit potentially slower, growth ahead.

Strategic Recommendations

  • Monitor Overbought Conditions: Given the prevalence of overbought RSI readings, we recommend closely monitoring these positions for signs of reversal or loss of momentum. Consider setting trailing stops or evaluating partial profit-taking strategies, particularly for names that have experienced "monster runs" like NBIS.
  • Watch Key Support Levels: For stocks exhibiting strong bullish trends, identify and monitor key moving averages (e.g., 20-day, 50-day SMAs) as potential support levels. A break below these could signal a shift in short-term sentiment.
  • Evaluate AXON's Divergence: The conflicting technical signals in AXON (overbought RSI but bearish SMA crossover) warrant extra scrutiny. We advise observing if the bullish momentum can overcome the intermediate-term bearish trend or if a pullback is imminent.
  • Diversification & Hedging: While the AI theme is powerful, maintaining a balanced portfolio and considering tactical hedges (e.g., inverse ETFs for the tech sector, or options strategies) could be prudent if market volatility increases or the AI rally shows signs of fatigue.
  • Long-Term Conviction: For core AI holdings, maintain conviction in the long-term growth thesis, but be prepared to manage short-term volatility.
